A vegan diet is a plant-based way of eating that excludes all animal-derived products. This means no meat, fish, dairy, eggs, or honey. Vegans rely entirely on foods that come from plants for their nutrition. The diet is followed for different reasons, including health, ethics, environmental sustainability, and personal beliefs. ğŸ¥— Foods Included in a Vegan Diet A healthy vegan diet includes a wide variety of plant foods:

1. Fruits

Fresh, dried, or frozen fruits provide vitamins, minerals, and natural sugars. Apples, bananas, berries, oranges, mangoes Rich in vitamin C, antioxidants, and fiber

2. Vegetables

Vegetables form the foundation of a vegan diet. Leafy greens (spinach, kale, lettuce) Root vegetables (carrots, potatoes, beets) Cruciferous vegetables (broccoli, cauliflower)

3. Grains

Whole grains are the main energy source. Rice, oats, wheat, barley, quinoa Provide carbohydrates, fiber, and B vitamins

4. Legumes

Legumes are the primary protein source. Beans, lentils, chickpeas, peas High in protein, iron, and fiber

5. Nuts and Seeds

Healthy fats and minerals come from nuts and seeds. Almonds, walnuts, peanuts Flaxseeds, chia seeds, sunflower seeds

6. Plant-Based Alternatives
